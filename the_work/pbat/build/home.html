Professional Services Firms

Many professional services firms have been leading the way for years in making their high-quality services available to the nonprofit sector. The immense pool of talent and best practices that these firms have to offer could transform the nonprofit sector through pro bono service.

Corporations

Corporations house a large pool of talent across a wide spectrum of professional skills needed by the nonprofit sector. Through pro bono service companies can realize both business and community benefits by donating their #1 asset - their human capital - to provide critically needed services to support to their communities.

Trade Associations

In today's marketplace where employees frequently transition between employers, they typically turn to professional societies and trade associations to stay connected to their profession throughout their careers. By promoting pro bono service to their members, these organizations are uniquely positioned to ingrain pro bono service in their respective professions, directing millions of dollars worth of critically needed professional services to the nonprofit sector.

Professional Schools

As of today, 145 of 200 law schools have a formal pro bono program and 95% of Deans agree that "it is an important goal of law schools to instill in students a sense of obligation to perform pro bono work during their later careers." Other professional schools are following suit and are well positioned to foster an equally strong pro bono ethic in their students.
