The Wedding

Our wedding will be held at the Cambridge Multicultural Arts Center (or CMAC), located in East Cambridge, Massachusetts. Designed by renown Boston architect Charles Bulfinch in 1814, the building opened in 1889 as the Cambridge City Courthouse. The city government has since outgrown its digs and moved a few blocks over, and the CMAC took over in 1978.

We love Boston and want you to have a great time while you're here.

Here are some pretty cool things to do.

Wandering (stuff you could walk to)

From the hotels in Cambridge, you'll be adjacent to the Charles River and (on the Boston side) the lovely Esplanade. Though the Fourth of July crowds will have (thankfully) dispersed, it's still one of the most peaceful, lovely places in town for a stroll.

Once you're on the Boston side of the river, you should pop over and check out the Boston Common. Centrally located to fancy shopping, Boston's downtown, and Beacon Hill, the Common acted as campgrounds for American soldiers heading into historic Revolutionary War battles. Nowadays, it's lovingly maintained by the City and is always full of flowers and swan boats.

It's been confirmed that the Red Sox will be out of town the weekend of the wedding. On the one hand, this is kind of a drag, but it probably works to our advantage; there will be 1,000,000 fewer people and cars in the city.

Shopping

Our hotels, and the wedding venue itself, are located just blocks away from the Cambridgeside Galleria, the largest proper mall in the area. That said, it's boring. It just has the same stores as every other mall in the world. For a more unique experience, head east a couple blocks to the Cambridge Antiques Market, a five-story monolith of a building packed with art, furniture, collectibles, ephemera, and a good bit of junk. Kenji bought Lauren her oldey-timey bike (a 1964 British steel-frame affair) from the bike shop in the basement here; you may have seen it in some of the engagement photos.

Also recommended is the entire stretch of Newbury Street. Kenji works here (and lived nearby for years) and can testify to the sheer number of shops, salons, and restaurants that line each block. It seems expensive (and it is, at times), but there's loads of deals to be had.

Food

Boston and Cambridge feature serious, world-class cuisine, at any price or fanciness level you desire. We eat out a lot around here, and these are our personal recommendations.

Cambridge: Hungry Mother (a brilliant, tiny place; Southern food with French preparation), Anna's Taqueria (everyone's favorite quick burrito/taco shop), Helmand (incredible Afghani cuisine; reputedly run by the elder brother of Afghan President Hamid Karzai), Moody's Falafel Palace (possibly the best falafel in town), Cuchi Cuchi (international food on shared plates (like tapas); the restaurant has a wonderful 1920s nightlife vibe).

Boston: The Upper Crust (by the slice or whole, it's the best thin-crust pizza in town), Café Jaffa (affordable, delicious Middle Eastern sandwiches and plates), UBurger (Boston's answer to California's In-N-Out burger; if you eat at the McDonald's across the street instead, you are no longer invited to our wedding), O Ya (absurdly expensive sushi, but so so mind-blowing), Osushi (cheaper, more accessible sushi (still not "cheap" though)).

Nightlife

We don't exactly stay up all night dancing and jumping from club to club, but we do get out a fair bit. I mean, come on, we're not married yet.

Cambridge: East Cambridge, where the hotels are, isn't terribly hopping with nightlife, but if you head west, you'll bump into some good times. Over on Massachusetts Avenue, south of Central Square, you can find Miracle of Science (a lightly nerdy bar on MIT campus; their menu is arranged as the periodic table), Middlesex Lounge (usually techno and electronic music; a long line Fri and Sat nights), the aforementioned Cuchi Cuchi, and The Enormous Room (a loungey space with big couches and tapestries on the floor for hanging out). You'll also find The Middle East and TT the Bear's - two of the best venues in town for live music. Check their schedules to see if anyone you like is playing that night.

Boston: The newly-renovated Liberty Hotel, located at the top of Charles Street, offers a mix of modern architecture and, uh, prison chic (it used to be a jail). Around the corner (on Cambridge Street) is Harvard Gardens, where a bunch of our friends used to work. Further down Charles street is one of our favorite dive bars, The Sevens. Cash only and no liquor license, so it's beer and wine only.
